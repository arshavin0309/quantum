Indices

Stock indices (often called market indices) are indicators of stock markets. They can consist of shares from companies in specific market sectors (for example, the NASDAQ), or from the largest corporations in a country. Among the latter are the American S&P 500, the British FTSE 100, and the Japanese Nikkei 225.

Commodities

Investing in commodities traded via contracts is a reliable way to mitigate risk, even amid inflation or economic uncertainty. These contracts protect both buyers and sellers from significant losses due to price fluctuations.
