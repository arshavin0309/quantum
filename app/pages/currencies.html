Frequently asked questions

  • What is Forex trading?

    The foreign exchange (Forex) market is a global, decentralized marketplace for currency exchange. It is the largest financial market in the world with a daily turnover of approximately $5 trillion, which is dozens of times greater than stock exchange volumes. For comparison, the New York Stock Exchange has a daily turnover of only about $169 billion. Key features of the Forex market include 24/5 operation and high liquidity, enabling instant buying and selling of currency pairs (e.g., EUR/USD or GBP/JPY). are its 24/5 operation and high liquidity, enabling instant buying and selling of currency pairs (e.g., EUR/USD or GBP/JPY).

  • How does Forex CFD trading work?

    A CFD (Contract for Difference) is an agreement to exchange the difference in the price of a currency pair between the opening and closing of a trade, without physically owning the underlying assets. The principle is simple: you either profit or lose based on the price difference at the time you enter and exit the trade. If the price moves in your favor, the CFD seller pays you the difference. If it moves against you, you compensate the seller for the loss. For example, if you buy a EUR/USD CFD at 1.1000 and close the trade at 1.1050, you will earn 50 pips. CFDs allow you to profit from both rising and falling markets and use leverage to amplify positions.

  • What are the advantages of Forex CFD trading?

    Accessibility: Start with small amounts (from $100).

    Two-sided market: Profit from both bullish and bearish trends (long or short).

    Leverage: Increase position sizes while maintaining a small deposit (risks also increase).

  • Which currency pairs are best for beginners?

    Beginners should focus on major pairs — the most liquid with tight spreads:

    EUR/USD (Euro/US Dollar): Low volatility

    GBP/USD (British Pound/US Dollar): Sensitive to news

    USD/JPY (US Dollar/Japanese Yen): Influenced by Bank of Japan policies

  • How to analyze the market?

    Use two approaches:

    Technical analysis: Study charts, indicators (MACD, RSI), and price patterns.

    Fundamental analysis: Monitor economic news (interest rates, GDP, inflation).

  • How to manage risks?

    Always set Stop Loss orders (e.g., 1-2% of your account per trade).

    Avoid excessive leverage (for beginners, no more than 1:10).

    Diversify your portfolio (trade not just Forex but also stocks and commodities).
