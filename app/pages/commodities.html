What are сommodities?

A market index is a composite benchmark that tracks the performance of a specific market segment or economic sector. It aggregates the value of its constituent stocks, typically calculated as a weighted average, providing a snapshot of overall market trends.

  • Broad exposure: Gain instant diversification across entire industries (e.g., tech, energy) without buying individual stocks.
  • Strategic trading: Profit from macroeconomic trends rather than company-specific news.

Key features of indices:

  • Liquidity: Tight spreads on major indices (e.g., S&P 500).
  • Volatility opportunities: React to GDP reports, interest rate releases, and other geopolitical events.
  • ETF/CFD access: Trade indices as derivatives without owning the underlying assets.
